About Us

Astronomy club is more a gathering of like-minded people brought together by the common passion for astronomy than a formal organisation. Here we organize all sort of amateur astronomical activities and also regular discussions on the topics of the world and beyond. We organise various theoretical and observational sessions to bring out the curious astronomer in every person. We also participate in various activities in different colleges.

Vision

When many people picture an astronomer at work, they think of someone huddling in a cold, dark observatory, squinting through a telescope. Nothing can be a graver understatement. Astronomy is undoubtedly the vastest field ever possible. From a tiny satellite to super-massive black holes, we worship them all.

But for such a vast field, prerequisites are surprisingly tiny. In astronomy club, we ask for only two things:

1. Passion for the Unknown: When you look up at the night sky, you see a very particular view of the Universe. There are stars, planets, supernova, nebulae, and so much more. Each wavelength tells its own tale of the universe. This is our passion. We just don't look at the stars for sheer feeling of pleasure and awe, but with hungry eyes, try to find the reason behind it, listening to all the tales the skies have to tell.

2. A curious mind: All that is needed is curiosity. Curiosity about what lies beyond the stars, or how big the universe is and what our place in it is. Are we alone or are we being watched? What is it that really lies within a black-hole? Why is dark matter invisible or why is anti-matter so repulsive?

The mystery that perpetually surrounds us makes us feel alive. Astronomy Club is a relatively young club with a passionate following. Our vision is to keep on expanding our reaches to expose the masses to the incredible life of an astronomer and the thrills of it.

Achievements

  • Nikhil Khatavkar and Vivek Gupta, Secretary and Joint-Secretary respectively of the club, came in top five at Inter IIT Technical Meet, 2014, held at IIT Bombay.
  • At Techkriti 2015, IIT Kanpur, Akash Sahu and Gaurav Tomar, the current Secretary and JointSecretary of the club, got first position in the Observational and Messier Hunt event of the fest.
  • And the team of Rajat Sable and Shubham Bhomle were the runners up of the Astro-Quiz event at Techkriti 2015, IIT Kanpur.
  • Our team consisting of 3 members from IIT(BHU) won the Messier Hunt Competition held at LNMIIT, Jaipur while the other team finished in top 6.

Projects

  • Club organised a number of workshops discussing various fields of Astronomy. They included Universe Formation , Dark Matter and Dark Energy, Galaxies and Galaxy formation, Solar System and Stellar Evolution.
  • Club also organised a number of observational workshops guiding the attendees regarding sky coordinate system and mapping of stars needed for star gazing. The young minds were given insight into telescope handling.

  • Last year, we also started Astrophotography in our club which involved capturing photographs of various phenomena and astronomical objects with a camera with or without a telescope. We managed to capture some beautiful pictures of Star Trail, Moon, Jupiter,Orion Nebula and Pleides.
